The Confluence district

As early as 1771, the engineer Michel-Antoine Perrache envisaged major works that would allow the confluence of the Rhône and the Saône, then located in the Ainay district, to be pushed further south of Lyon. At the time of his death in 1779, the Perrache company continued the work, which was not completed until 1841. Taking advantage of the development of the railway, a first station was built in the Perrache district in 1846, while waiting for the current station built in 1857. This new dynamic led to the development of the district, around industrial and port activities. Since the 2000s, the decision has been taken to take advantage of this area of industrial wasteland to expand the city centre of Lyon. The Confluence rehabilitation programme is based on housing, office and leisure projects. This contemporary architecture district is currently being developed and features the work of some of the biggest names in contemporary architecture. Examples include the Hôtel de Région by Christian de Portzamparc, the orange cube by Jakob + MacFarlane, and the leisure centre by Jean-Paul Viguier. Coop Himmelb(l)au's Musée des Confluences, at the tip of the confluence, is a new landmark for the city. A dance centre should also open in 2018. This district also offers green spaces and walks along the banks of the Saône or around the nautical square.
